I am a first-year engineering student at McMaster University with research and design experience spanning robotics, computational biology, and artificial intelligence. My technical interests centre on robotics systems engineering — from mechanical design and CAD to perception, grasp planning, and robot operating frameworks — with a focus on applications in space, defence, and healthcare contexts where engineering complexity serves a clear purposeful mission.

I am an undergraduate researcher at the McMaster Robotics and Manipulation Lab (RMAL), where my work sits at the intersection of manipulation and intelligent systems. In parallel, I contribute to lupus research at McMaster's Health Sciences faculty — a position I was invited to join based on my prior independent research — and serve as Founder and Head of Aletheia, an independent research initiative I established to pursue original scientific work outside of institutional boundaries.

At the international level, I represented Canada at the Intel International Science and Engineering Fair (ISEF) 2024, placing 4th in Health Sciences for original work in computational immunogenomics. I am a Schulich Leader Nominee and have accumulated over 30 awards across five years of competition at BASEF. I am also an active member of McMaster's University Rover Challenge (URC) team, where I independently designed and built mechanical systems for a Mars-analog rover.

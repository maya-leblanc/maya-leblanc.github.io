Designed primary and secondary GPS antenna mounts for McMaster's URC Mars Rover. The two mounts are positioned approximately 1 metre apart on the rover chassis, providing spatial baseline separation for antenna diversity and improved GPS signal reliability in the field.

The 1-metre baseline separation between primary and secondary antennas implements spatial antenna diversity — a standard technique for improving signal reliability in environments with multipath interference, such as the rocky desert terrain of the URC competition site.

**Tools:** SolidWorks, FDM 3D printing (PLA), carbon fibre mast.
